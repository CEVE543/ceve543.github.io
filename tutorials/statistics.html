Statistics references

There are lots of great resources beyond this website. Here are some especially good ones.

  • Gelman et al. (2014) is a classical and detailed textbook on Bayesian inference. Chapter 1 may be helpful background reading if you’re feeling a bit shaky about your statistics.
  • Gelman (2021) is a less technical textbook with clear and well-worked examples (mostly not environmental).
  • (james_statlearn:2013?) is a classic introduction to machine learning, which complements the Bayesian perspective nicely.
  • Nazarathy & Klok (2021) offers some tutorials on using Julia for statistics and machine learning; reading good code is a great way to improve your coding.
  • For a fairly detailed exploration of hierarchical space-time models, see Cressie & Wikle (2011). There have been some computational advances since then that are worth keeping in mind before you apply these models directly, but it’s a clearly written overview.
  • The United States Geological Survey (USGS) uses England Jr. et al. (2019) for flood frequency analysis. As you can see, there is a tremendous gap between modern statistical theory and methodology, and what federal agencies require analysis to look like.
  • (helsel_waterresources:2020?) is a textbook also produced by the USGS covering some general statistical methods with a water resources focus.
  • The documentation for the Turing, PyMC, and (especially) stan probabilistic programming languages offer outstanding tutorials on statistical modeling
Gelman, A., Carlin, J. B., Stern, H. S., & Rubin, D. B. (2014). Bayesian Data Analysis (3rd ed.). Chapman & Hall/CRC Boca Raton, FL, USA.
Gelman, A. (2021). Regression and other stories. Cambridge, United Kingdom ; Cambridge University Press.
Nazarathy, Y., & Klok, H. (2021). Statistics with Julia: Fundamentals for Data Science, Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. Springer International Publishing. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-70901-3
Cressie, N. A. C., & Wikle, C. K. (2011). Statistics for spatio-temporal data. Hoboken, N.J.: Wiley.
England Jr., J. F., Cohn, T. A., Faber, B. A., Stedinger, J. R., Thomas Jr., W. O., Veilleux, A. G., et al. (2019). Guidelines for determining flood flow frequency Bulletin 17C. In Techniques and methods (Version 1.1, Vol. 4–B5, p. 148). U.S.Geological Survey.
